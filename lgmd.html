Limb-Girdle Muscular Dystrophy (LGMD)

A comprehensive deep dive into the complex sub-types, classifications, and latest care practices.

What is LGMD?

Limb-Girdle Muscular Dystrophy is not a single disease, but a large family of different genetic muscle disorders. It primary targets and weakens the voluntary muscles around the shoulders and the hips (pelvic girdle area).

Because the pelvic and shoulder muscles weaken over time, patients usually face difficulty standing up from chairs, climbing stairs, or lifting their arms to brush hair and carry objects. It can start at any age, depending heavily on the specific sub-type inherited.

Complete LGMD Family Map (All Known Sub-types)

LGMD is divided into two main scientific groups based on how it is passed down through families: LGMD1 (Dominant Type) and LGMD2 / LGMD R (Recessive Type).

Critical Care Highlights

The Power of Genetic Testing: Because LGMD has dozens of unique sub-types that look identical from the outside, a multi-gene NGS (Next-Generation Sequencing) DNA panel test is highly recommended to pinpoint the exact broken gene before starting any management path.

Heart & Respiratory Tracking: Certain variants of LGMD can silently affect the cardiac (heart) rhythms or weaken the diaphragm (breathing muscles). Annual echo-cardiograms and forced vital capacity (FVC) breathing tests are vital to prevent sudden complications.
